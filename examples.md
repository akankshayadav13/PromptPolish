# PromptPolish – Example Inputs & Outputs

This file demonstrates how PromptPolish transforms rough input text into
polished, tone-specific versions.

These examples help users understand how the system behaves in real scenarios.

---

# ⭐ Example 1: Friendly Tone

### **Input**
I want to ask the HR about my application status but I'm scared I’ll sound rude.

### **Output**
[Tone: friendly]

Hey! Here's a softer way to ask about your application status:

“Hi, I hope you're doing well. I just wanted to check in and see if there are any updates regarding my application. No rush — I appreciate your time!”

---

# ⭐ Example 2: Interview-Appropriate Tone

### **Input**
I like your company and want to join.

### **Output**
[Tone: interview-appropriate]

I’m genuinely interested in joining your organization because I admire your focus on innovation and long-term growth. I believe my curiosity and adaptability would help me contribute meaningfully to your team.

---

# ⭐ Example 3: Corporate Tone

### **Input**
send me the project details fast.

### **Output**
[Tone: corporate]

Could you please share the project details at your earliest convenience? This will help me proceed with the next steps promptly.

---

# ⭐ Example 4: Assertive Tone

### **Input**
I already submitted the document. Why are you asking again?

### **Output**
[Tone: assertive]

I’ve already submitted the document earlier. Could you please confirm if it was received, or let me know if you need it in a different format?

---

# ⭐ Example 5: Empathetic Tone

### **Input**
I messed up my interview, I don’t think I can ever get a job.

### **Output**
[Tone: empathetic]

I’m really sorry you’re feeling this way. One tough interview doesn’t define your abilities — it’s just one moment. You’re learning, improving, and getting stronger with every step. You’re not alone in this.

---

These examples show how PromptPolish adapts the same input into different tones
while keeping the meaning intact.
